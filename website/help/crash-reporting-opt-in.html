Crash reporting

Crash reporting is off by default.

You choose whether Enigma Memory can send safe crash diagnostics. Crash reports are designed to exclude memory, prompts, wallet data, and local paths.

What is sent if you opt in

When crash reporting is on and the app panics, Enigma sends a small report that contains only:

  • The Enigma Memory app version.
  • Your operating-system family, such as Windows, macOS, or Linux.
  • A coarse timestamp, rounded to the nearest hour.
  • The first line of the panic message, with no stack trace beyond the originating module name.

This information is enough for the team to spot regressions across platforms and releases without exposing anything personal.

What is never sent

Crash reports never include:

  • Memory contents, prompts, transcripts, or provider responses.
  • Wallet addresses, private keys, or credentials of any kind.
  • Local file paths, vault locations, or account identifiers.
  • Full stack traces, heap dumps, or log files.
Boundary note: Crash reports are designed to be shareable with support without redaction. If you are ever unsure, keep crash reporting off and use the safe support sharing guide instead.

How to opt in or out

  1. Open Enigma Memory.
  2. Go to Settings > Support.
  3. Toggle Send crash reports on or off.

The change takes effect immediately. If you turn crash reporting off, any pending reports stay on your device and are not transmitted.

Send pending reports

If a crash happened while reporting was off, or if the report could not be sent immediately, Enigma keeps a pending report locally. When you opt in, a Send pending reports button appears in Settings > Support.

Clicking it sends only the pending reports that were created after you enabled crash reporting. You can review the count before confirming.

Before you send anything else

For screenshots, copied diagnostics, or support emails, use the safe support sharing guide. Do not include raw memory text, prompts, transcripts, credentials, account IDs, or local file paths.
